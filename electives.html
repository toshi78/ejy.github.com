Elective Placements

Medsin Birmingham Electives www.medsin-birmingham.org.uk

Are you looking for an elective placement over seas? Would you like to go somewhere challenging and exciting but with the back-up of an experienced team behind you? Then you should consider an elective with Health poverty Action.

This year Health Poverty Action (HPA), through partnership with Medsin Birmingham, are offering elective placements to Birmingham medical students. HPA have been working for over 25 years in troubled countries around the world. They know the regions well and have excellent relationships with the locals, to give you the safest, most productive elective available.
Contact Lizzie Helps (Outreach Fundraising Officer) - 020 7840 3762 for more information

Health Poverty Action is currently offering Medical Elective placements in the following countries:

  • Sierra Leone (a large, busy, under resourced hospital- not for the faint hearted!)
  • Laos (community-outreach work)
  • Nicaragua (a small health centre)

Placements are tailored to the individual student and their interests and the cost varies depending on where they go and for how long. HPA ask for a contribution towards their accommodation, travel in country and support from their staff throughout.

Search the elective FAQs to find out more, the BMA toolkit/guidelines or email Medsin for more information.
