Louise Roper (President)

Hi, I’m Louise, president of Medsin. Medsin is a great charity to get involved in because we do a bit of everything – we get involved with lots of other charities (like Teddy Bear Hospital, Sexpressions and many more) and we do our own thing (like campaigning for Student Stop AIDS, work with Health Poverty Action, hosting lectures from world-famous speakers and reacting to global health crises). So if you’re keen to make a difference and want an exciting project to get involved in, contact Medsin.

Phillip Rankin (Secretary)

Hello, I’m Phil and I’m Medsin’s Secretary. I have been in this role for two years now and have really enjoyed taking part in the development of this exciting charity and documenting everything Medsin has achieved. One of the highlights for me was one of our Global Health Lectures last year when Jonathan Glennie from UCL gave an outstanding talk called ‘The Trouble with Aid’ to a full lecture theatre. Also I like how Medsin benefits from being an ‘umbrella charity’ for all other charities in the medical school. Thanks to this, we have the potential to work with each of the charities and there is lots of opportunity to bring all our different campaigns together.
